Alternative Methods
 We’ve outlined three main ways that you can build and run greener websites. But what about when you’re simply surfing the web for enjoyment or work? Here are some tips for staying greener in your everyday life on the web: Use an alternative search engine such as Ecosia, which uses its profits to plant trees and runs on 100% renewable energy. Maybe don’t send that polite but essentially useless “Thank you” email – think before you send, or go to talk to that person face to face! One email emits 10g of CO2 – that means a single email has the same carbon footprint as a standard plastic bag! Lower your monitor brightness levels, or use dark mode, to reduce the amount of power needed to light up your screen. Download Netflix shows instead of streaming them – as well as saving a bit of energy, it also means you’ll be entertained even when you have no signal! Go directly to websites instead of Googling them first. A lot of the time, we use Google like an online taxi to quickly take us exactly where we want to go. Instead of typing in “Twitter” – or even “Website Builder Expert” – save your most-visited sites to your toolbar, or enter the URL directly to save energy!
